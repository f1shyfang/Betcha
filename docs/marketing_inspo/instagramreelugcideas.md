# UGC Ideas For Hackerhouse

## 1. "Things friends already bet on"
Hook: "We built an app for the stuff friends already argue about."
Shots: quick cuts of gym streaks, chores, game night picks, and group chat debates.
Angle: show that Betcha turns existing friend behavior into a product.
CTA: "What would you bet your friends on?"

## 2. "Make ordinary life feel expensive"
Hook: "What if gym consistency and roommate chores had real stakes?"
Shots: someone staring at a calendar, marking a gym day, then a fake dramatic reveal of the app.
Angle: contrast boring everyday moments with playful tension.
CTA: "Betcha is the product for your friend group drama."

## 3. "POV: your friend group needed consequences"
Hook: "POV: your friend group finally got accountability."
Shots: text message screenshots, a prediction being placed, then a reaction after resolution.
Angle: lean into the social utility and humor.
CTA: "Send this to the friend who always says they’ll show up."

## 4. "Build in public at Hackerhouse"
Hook: "We spent Hackerhouse building the weirdest useful thing we've made."
Shots: desk footage, whiteboard sketches, laptop builds, a demo screen recording.
Angle: show momentum, urgency, and maker energy.
CTA: "Would you use this with your friends?"

## 5. "The one-line pitch"
Hook: "Betcha is a private prediction market for friends."
Shots: founder speaking directly to camera, app screen, quick examples of bets.
Angle: keep it clean and repeatable for a short reel.
CTA: "If this makes sense to you, it probably makes sense to your group chat too."

## 6. "Before and after Betcha"
Hook: "Before Betcha: 'I swear I’ll do it.' After Betcha: receipts."
Shots: first half shows lazy promises, second half shows prediction entry and resolution.
Angle: transformation format works well for short-form.
CTA: "Every group chat needs a little accountability."

## 7. "Why this is fun"
Hook: "This is not really a finance app. It is a social game."
Shots: laughter, friend reactions, leaderboard view, reaction shots.
Angle: frame the emotional core, not the mechanism.
CTA: "The stakes are light, but the energy is real."

## 8. "Three use cases in 15 seconds"
Hook: "3 ways people would actually use this:"
Shots: 1) gym consistency, 2) roommate chores, 3) game night predictions.
Angle: fast, practical, easy to understand.
CTA: "Which one would your friend group use first?"

## 9. "Founder confession"
Hook: "We built this because our friend groups already did it manually."
Shots: founder talking to camera, screenshots of group chats, mock bet examples.
Angle: make the idea feel obvious and human.
CTA: "Sometimes the best products just formalize what people already do."

## 10. "Launch reaction"
Hook: "The first thing people said after seeing Betcha: 'wait, I would use this.'"
Shots: reaction-style clips, screen recording, fast social proof text overlays.
Angle: capture curiosity and validation.
CTA: "That was the signal we wanted."

## Fast Production Formats
- Talking head + screen recording.
- Photo dump reel with text overlays.
- Friend reaction clips.
- Screen-recorded demo with subtitles.
- Whiteboard-to-product build montage.

## Caption Starters
- "We built Betcha during Hackerhouse because ordinary moments are more fun with stakes."
- "A private prediction market for friends. That is the whole idea."
- "This started as a joke and turned into a product."
- "The best products often come from behavior people already have."

## Best CTA Options
- "What would you bet your friends on?"
- "Which friend group would use this first?"
- "Would you use this for gym, chores, or game night?"
- "Send this to the friend who always needs consequences."
