## Draft 2: More personal and energetic
We spent Hackerhouse building Betcha, a private prediction market for friends.

The idea is simple: people already make bets with each other all the time. Gym streaks, chores, game night, who will show up, who will follow through. Betcha gives those moments a place to live.

Instead of treating every bet like a transaction, we wanted to make it feel social. Lightweight stakes. Private invite links. Simple resolution. A reason to come back next week and do it again.

What we’re learning is that the real product is not the market itself. It is the ritual around it. When the stakes are small but real, people pay more attention, joke more, and actually follow through.

Still early, but this has been a fun build and a strong reminder that some of the best consumer products start with very human behavior.

One of the most interesting things we learned at Hackerhouse is that the best product ideas are often hiding inside the ordinary.

That is what led us to Betcha, a private prediction market for friends. It is built around a simple belief: if you add a little stake to everyday moments, people care more, laugh more, and follow through more often.
